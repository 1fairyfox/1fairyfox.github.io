A **farm** is a mesh project that is a *growing collection* tended on a schedule — new items
planted periodically, existing items grown a little every day — rather than a single fixed
release. Fairy Fox Games and Fairy Fox Stories are the two farms; they share this operating
model even though the craft differs. The canonical copy is in the repository at
`hub/standards/farm-operating-model.md`; it governs the integrated farm tier, which renders as
pages of fairyfox.io under the **Farms** nav slot.

## The rules

- **Grow daily; plant periodically.** The daily job *tends the collection* — it deepens at least
  one existing item each run, not only adds new ones. New items are planted on a slower cadence.
  Growth, not just breadth.
- **Items are first-class citizens, not throwaway demos.** Every item earns ongoing investment —
  maintenance, docs, tests, real growth.
- **Every item carries real content and craft.** No filler, no near-identical padding. If it
  isn't good, it doesn't ship.
- **New items are genuinely distinct — never a re-skin.** Each is a mechanically or creatively
  distinct experiment; check the existing set first.
- **Simple-but-deep is the hard constraint.** Growth must never make an item convoluted or
  cluttered. Depth lives in the mechanic or the story, not in clutter.
- **Plan-first.** An item begins from a plan appropriate to its craft — a game concept, or a
  book's full public blueprint before a word of prose.
